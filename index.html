Common Questions

Everything you need to know before starting your Mauritius journey

Mauritius offers several residency pathways: the Premium Visa (up to 1 year, renewable, for remote workers and retirees), the Occupation Permit (for professionals and self-employed), the Investor Permit (minimum USD 50,000 investment), and the Retired Non-Citizen Permit (for those 50+ with a monthly income transfer). We assess your profile and recommend the most suitable route.

The Premium Visa is typically processed within 2–4 weeks. Occupation and Investor Permits take 4–8 weeks once a complete application is submitted. We prepare your documents meticulously to avoid delays. Most clients receive approvals within their expected timelines.

Yes. Foreigners can purchase property in Mauritius through approved schemes: PDS (Property Development Scheme), IRS (Integrated Resort Scheme), RES (Real Estate Scheme), and apartments above the 2nd floor in certain developments. A purchase of USD 375,000+ under PDS automatically qualifies the buyer for a Permanent Residence Permit. We work with licensed property advisors to guide your purchase.

Mauritius has a flat 15% income tax rate, no capital gains tax, no inheritance tax, and no withholding tax on dividends. Tax residency requires spending 183+ days in the country. With 45+ double tax treaties, many residents can significantly reduce their global tax burden. We connect you with qualified tax advisors as part of our service.

Popular expat areas include Grand Baie (vibrant, cosmopolitan, northern coast), Tamarin / Black River (surf community, relaxed lifestyle), Flic en Flac (affordable, beach-focused), Pereybere (family-friendly, calm), and Bel Ombre (luxury estates, southern coast). The right location depends on your lifestyle, family needs, and budget — our advisors provide tailored neighbourhood guidance.

Cost of living varies widely. A comfortable single expat lifestyle costs €1,500–€2,500/month. A family with international schooling and a good villa typically budgets €4,000–€8,000/month. Property rentals range from €600/month for an apartment to €5,000+/month for a luxury beachfront villa. Groceries, dining, and utilities are generally more affordable than Western Europe.

Key institutions include Northfields International High School, Westcoast International School, École du Nord, and others.
