Business Setup

Company Formation Types

Mauritius offers multiple corporate structures. We help you choose the right one for your activities and tax objectives.

Domestic Company

For local operations

A private limited company conducting business primarily within Mauritius. Ideal for retail, hospitality, professional services, and locally-focused ventures.

  • 15% flat corporate tax rate
  • No minimum capital requirement
  • Incorporated in 5–10 business days
  • One resident director required
Most Popular

Global Business Licence (GBL)

For international operations

The preferred structure for businesses with international clients, investment activities, or holding companies. Qualifies for Mauritius's full network of double tax treaties.

  • 15% corporate tax (may be reduced by treaties)
  • Access to 45+ double tax treaties
  • No withholding tax on dividends
  • FSC licence required

Authorised Company

For non-resident businesses

A lighter-touch structure for businesses that are managed and controlled outside Mauritius but wish to use the island as a base. Simple compliance requirements.

  • Exempt from Mauritius income tax
  • No treaty access
  • Minimal local compliance
  • Quick and low-cost to set up
Tax Efficiency

Mauritius Tax Benefits

One of Africa's most attractive tax regimes for individuals and businesses alike.

15%

Flat Income Tax

A single flat rate for both personal and corporate income. No progressive brackets, no surprises.

0%

Capital Gains Tax

No tax on gains from the disposal of shares, property, or other capital assets. Ideal for investors.

0%

Inheritance Tax

No estate duty or inheritance tax. Wealth can pass to heirs without additional tax burden.

45+

Tax Treaties

Mauritius has double tax avoidance agreements with over 45 countries, covering most major economies.

Tax Residency: Achieved by spending 183+ days per year in Mauritius, or 270 days over 3 years.
No Withholding Tax: Dividends, interest, and royalties paid to non-residents are generally exempt from withholding tax.
Territorial Tax: Only Mauritius-sourced income is subject to tax for non-domiciled residents in certain structures.

Why Get This Right from Day One?

Restructuring an incorrectly set-up company after the fact is costly, time-consuming, and may trigger tax events.

Using the wrong company type means you may miss out on treaty benefits or face unexpected withholding taxes on distributions.

Mauritius's tax authority (MRA) increasingly scrutinises substance requirements — getting proper advice upfront ensures genuine compliance.

The right structure protects you, your family, and your assets — from liability, currency risk, and cross-border tax exposure.
